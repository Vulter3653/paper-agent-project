# 7. Limitations and Ethics

The current evidence has clear boundaries. Full T004-T020 validation remains incomplete. D1 batch-aware persistence is not implemented. Phase 3L recorded a T007 timeout after approximately 250 seconds, and T013-T020 were not executed under Phase 3L after the safety stop. Gold-label coverage remains limited and should be expanded before making broader performance claims.

Paper Agent also introduces normative choices. The internal journal allowlist can create algorithmic gatekeeping by prioritizing established venues and potentially excluding useful work from emerging, regional, or interdisciplinary outlets. Automated ranking can amplify citation and journal-hierarchy biases. DOI verification improves metadata integrity but does not establish substantive research quality. Open-access status can improve accessibility but should not be interpreted as a quality signal.

Human scholarly review remains the final authority. Users must inspect recommendations, verify metadata when necessary, evaluate methodological fit, and take responsibility for citation choices. The system should present uncertainty and failure evidence rather than conceal them. Responsibility for hallucination, metadata error, and ranking bias cannot be delegated entirely to an automated pipeline.
